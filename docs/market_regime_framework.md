# PORTFOLIO OS — MARKET REGIME FRAMEWORK
Version: v1
Status: Canonical Regime SSOT

---

# PURPOSE

This document defines how Portfolio OS interprets market regimes.

A market regime is not a market prediction.

A market regime is the current structural condition of:

- liquidity
- participation
- volatility
- macro pressure
- cross-asset behavior
- market leadership
- risk appetite

The purpose of the regime framework is to explain:

"What type of market environment currently exists?"

---

# CORE PRINCIPLE

Market regimes are structural environments.

Regimes are NOT:

- headlines
- emotions
- predictions
- short-term narratives
- social-media sentiment

Regimes emerge from:

- measurable market structure
- cross-confirmed signals
- semantic interpretation
- structural consistency

---

# REGIME CHAIN

Portfolio OS follows this chain:

Raw Market Signals
→ Semantic Regime States
→ Regime Interpretation
→ Portfolio Impact
→ PM Reasoning
→ Action Space

No regime conclusion may bypass signal structure.

---

# REGIME OBJECTIVE

The regime system should answer:

- What environment currently dominates?
- Which conditions support risk assets?
- Which conditions weaken resilience?
- Which exposures currently benefit?
- Which exposures become fragile?
- Which market structures are changing?

---

# REGIME DIMENSIONS

Portfolio OS evaluates multiple regime dimensions simultaneously.

---

# 1. LIQUIDITY CONDITIONS

Purpose:
Measure market support conditions.

Examples:

- supportive liquidity
- tightening liquidity
- deteriorating liquidity

Signals include:

- yields
- credit conditions
- rate expectations
- volatility structure

Questions answered:

- Are markets structurally supported?
- Are financial conditions tightening?

---

# 2. PARTICIPATION CONDITIONS

Purpose:
Measure market breadth and participation quality.

Examples:

- participation_broad
- participation_narrow

Signals include:

- breadth
- sector participation
- index leadership concentration

Questions answered:

- Is the market broadly healthy?
- Or driven by a narrow leadership group?

---

# 3. VOLATILITY CONDITIONS

Purpose:
Measure structural market stress.

Examples:

- volatility_stable
- volatility_elevated

Signals include:

- VIX behavior
- realized volatility
- cross-asset stress

Questions answered:

- Is market stress rising?
- Is risk appetite weakening?

---

# 4. CROSS-ASSET CONDITIONS

Purpose:
Evaluate macro consistency.

Examples:

- equities rising while yields stable
- defensive divergence
- credit deterioration

Signals include:

- equities
- bonds
- yields
- credit spreads
- commodities
- currencies

Questions answered:

- Do assets confirm each other?
- Or does structural divergence emerge?

---

# 5. LEADERSHIP CONDITIONS

Purpose:
Evaluate which themes dominate markets.

Examples:

- AI leadership
- defensive rotation
- cyclical participation
- quality dominance

Questions answered:

- What currently drives market strength?
- Which narratives dominate flows?

---

# REGIME STATES

Portfolio OS currently recognizes multiple regime states.

---

# market_regime_risk_on

Meaning:

- supportive liquidity
- broad participation
- stable volatility
- healthy cross-asset confirmation

Implications:

- growth exposure supported
- deployment conditions improve
- risk appetite healthy

---

# market_regime_neutral

Meaning:

- mixed structural conditions
- stable but selective environment
- no dominant stress structure

Implications:

- selectivity becomes important
- concentration requires monitoring
- broad conviction weakens

---

# market_regime_fragile

Meaning:

- increasing divergence
- narrowing participation
- concentration escalation
- weakening confirmation

Implications:

- structural fragility increases
- resilience importance rises
- dependency chains become critical

---

# market_regime_risk_off

Meaning:

- deteriorating liquidity
- rising stress
- weak participation
- defensive positioning dominance

Implications:

- resilience dominates growth
- diversification importance rises
- exposure discipline critical

---

# REGIME INTERPRETATION RULE

The system must ALWAYS explain:

- why the regime exists
- which signals created the regime
- what structurally supports the regime
- what weakens the regime

Bad Example:

"Neutral regime."

Good Example:

"Liquidity conditions remain stable,
while market participation stays relatively healthy.
However, leadership remains concentrated,
which creates a selective but not fully risk-on environment."

---

# REGIME CONFLICT RULE

Conflicting conditions reduce regime clarity.

Example:

- supportive liquidity
- narrowing participation
- rising volatility

This creates a fragile regime structure.

The system must:

- reduce confidence
- widen action-space
- acknowledge ambiguity

The system must NEVER force false regime certainty.

---

# REGIME PRIORITY MODEL

Regime interpretation prioritizes:

1. Liquidity
2. Cross-asset confirmation
3. Breadth quality
4. Volatility structure
5. Leadership concentration
6. Tactical momentum

Structural conditions dominate headlines.

---

# PORTFOLIO RELATIONSHIP

The same regime affects portfolios differently.

Example:

Risk-on regime may support:

- growth-heavy portfolios

while simultaneously increasing:

- concentration fragility

The regime system must evaluate:

portfolio-specific implications.

---

# REGIME VS PREDICTION

Portfolio OS explicitly separates:

Regime Interpretation
from
Market Prediction.

Example:

The system may identify:

- supportive liquidity
- broad participation
- stable volatility

without claiming:

"Markets will definitely rise."

The system interprets structure,
not future certainty.
