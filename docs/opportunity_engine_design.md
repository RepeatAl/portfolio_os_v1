# PORTFOLIO OS — OPPORTUNITY ENGINE DESIGN
Version: v1
Status: Canonical Opportunity System SSOT

---

# PURPOSE

This document defines how the Opportunity Engine works inside Portfolio OS.

The Opportunity Engine does NOT exist to find:

- hype stocks
- trending assets
- speculative momentum
- social-media-driven trades

The Opportunity Engine exists to identify:

- structurally compatible opportunities
- portfolio-improving additions
- diversification improvements
- deployment candidates
- resilience-enhancing exposures
- asymmetry opportunities

The engine evaluates opportunities in portfolio context.

---

# CORE PRINCIPLE

An asset is NOT automatically an opportunity.

An opportunity only exists relative to:

- current portfolio structure
- current market regime
- current deployment conditions
- current exposure balance
- current dependency structure

Portfolio context determines opportunity quality.

---

# OPPORTUNITY CHAIN

Portfolio OS follows this chain:

Market Signals
→ Portfolio State
→ Structural Gaps
→ Opportunity Screening
→ Semantic Interpretation
→ PM Reasoning
→ Action Space

No opportunity may bypass portfolio context.

---

# OPPORTUNITY OBJECTIVE

The Opportunity Engine should answer:

- What currently improves portfolio structure?
- What reduces hidden fragility?
- What diversifies dependency?
- What improves resilience?
- What improves deployment quality?
- What strengthens structural balance?

The engine must NEVER optimize only for:

- hype
- momentum
- excitement
- short-term narrative popularity

---

# OPPORTUNITY TYPES

Portfolio OS recognizes multiple opportunity classes.

---

# TYPE 1 — DIVERSIFICATION OPPORTUNITIES

Purpose:
Reduce concentration and dependency.

Examples:

- adding healthcare exposure
- adding defensive diversification
- reducing AI narrative overlap
- broadening geographic exposure

Questions answered:

- What reduces concentration?
- What broadens participation?
- What weakens dependency chains?

---

# TYPE 2 — REGIME-ALIGNED OPPORTUNITIES

Purpose:
Align portfolio with current market structure.

Examples:

- liquidity-supported growth
- defensive exposure during stress
- quality cash flow during tightening

Questions answered:

- Which assets fit current regime conditions?
- Which exposures gain structural support?
- Which allocations become more resilient?

---

# TYPE 3 — DEPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITIES

Purpose:
Improve capital deployment quality.

Examples:

- staged deployment
- underinvested portfolio improvement
- capital efficiency enhancement

Questions answered:

- Where can idle capital improve structure?
- Which additions improve deployment balance?
- Which opportunities preserve flexibility?

---

# TYPE 4 — ASYMMETRIC OPPORTUNITIES

Purpose:
Identify structurally favorable risk/reward conditions.

Examples:

- underrepresented sectors
- structurally improving participation
- strengthening breadth conditions

Questions answered:

- Which opportunities improve upside asymmetry?
- Which positions improve structural balance?
- Which opportunities reduce fragility while maintaining upside?

---

# OPPORTUNITY INPUTS

The engine may evaluate:

- allocation gaps
- concentration pressure
- narrative dependency
- liquidity conditions
- breadth structure
- cross-asset signals
- valuation context
- macro regime
- deployment conditions
- portfolio health

The engine evaluates opportunity quality holistically.

---

# OPPORTUNITY FILTERING

Portfolio OS prioritizes:

- structural quality
- resilience contribution
- diversification value
- dependency reduction
- regime compatibility

The system deprioritizes:

- social hype
- emotional momentum
- crowded retail narratives
- speculative mania

---

# STRUCTURAL FIT PRINCIPLE

A good company is NOT automatically a good portfolio fit.

Example:

A portfolio already highly dependent on AI growth
may not structurally improve through additional AI exposure,
even if the company quality remains high.

Portfolio fit dominates isolated asset quality.

---

# OPPORTUNITY SEMANTICS

Opportunities must pass through semantic interpretation.

Examples:

- diversification_improving
- dependency_reducing
- liquidity_supported
- deployment_efficient
- resilience_enhancing
- concentration_increasing

Reports interpret semantic opportunity meaning,
not raw screening data alone.

---

# OPPORTUNITY SCORING MODEL

Opportunity quality should evaluate:

1. Structural improvement
2. Diversification impact
3. Dependency impact
4. Regime compatibility
5. Deployment efficiency
6. Liquidity compatibility
7. Scenario resilience

Portfolio contribution matters more than isolated momentum.

---

# OPPORTUNITY GOVERNANCE RULES

Portfolio OS must NEVER generate:

- aggressive buy signals
- emotional urgency
- "must buy now" language
- hype framing
- speculative conviction unsupported by structure

The engine must remain:

- explainable
- PM-oriented
- structurally grounded
- deterministic

---

# ACTION SPACE RELATIONSHIP

The Opportunity Engine generates possibilities,
not commands.

Examples:

Option A:
Increase healthcare diversification.

Option B:
Maintain current cash flexibility.

Option C:
Add selective exposure without increasing concentration.

The system supports PM reasoning,
not blind execution.

---

# CONFIDENCE RELATIONSHIP

Opportunity confidence depends on:

- signal alignment
- regime coherence
- breadth stability
- liquidity conditions
- scenario consistency

High opportunity confidence means:

"The opportunity structurally fits the current portfolio environment."

NOT:

"The asset is guaranteed to outperform."

---

# HUMAN READABILITY RULE

Opportunity explanations must remain understandable.

The system should explain:

- why something improves the portfolio
- what dependency changes
- what structural weakness improves
- what tradeoffs emerge

The system must avoid:

- institutional jargon
- vague PM language
- AI filler text
- prediction framing

---

# SIMULATION RELATIONSHIP

All opportunities should ideally support simulation.

The system should explain:

- before state
- after state
- structural changes
- resilience impact
- concentration changes
- deployment changes

Opportunities become more valuable when explainable through simulation.

---

# REPORT RELATIONSHIP

Reports explain opportunity meaning.

Reports should answer:

- why this opportunity matters
- what structural gap it addresses
- what improves
- what tradeoffs exist
- how portfolio posture changes

---

# DASHBOARD RELATIONSHIP

The dashboard visualizes:

- opportunity rankings
- structural fit
- diversification impact
- deployment quality
- resilience contribution

The dashboard visualizes reasoning outputs,
not raw screening noise.

---

# LONG-TERM OPPORTUNITY EXPANSION

Future opportunity systems may include:

- valuation integration
- earnings-quality overlays
- macro sensitivity scoring
- behavioral crowding analysis
- thematic cycle detection
- structural rotation analysis
- portfolio-fit ranking
- adaptive deployment sequencing

All future systems must remain:

- explainable
- deterministic
- signal-based
- structurally interpretable

---

# OPPORTUNITY PHILOSOPHY

Portfolio OS does not search for excitement.

Portfolio OS searches for:

- structural improvement
- resilience enhancement
- intelligent deployment
- portfolio balance
- explainable opportunity quality

Opportunity quality is determined by portfolio fit,
not by hype intensity.
