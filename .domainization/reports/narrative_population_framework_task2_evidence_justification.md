## Evidence Justification 3: GLP-1 / Obesity Medicine

**Task**: 2.3
**Proposed ID**: `narrative.glp1_obesity_medicine` (CANDIDATE reference — not a registry entry)

### Observed Facts Summary

- **FDA approvals**: Ozempic (semaglutide, T2D 2017; obesity indication via Wegovy 2021), Mounjaro (tirzepatide, T2D 2022), Zepbound (tirzepatide, obesity 2023)
- **Clinical trial results**: STEP trials (semaglutide: ~15% body weight loss), SURMOUNT trials (tirzepatide: ~20-22% body weight loss)
- **Prescription volume surge**: Exponential growth in GLP-1 prescriptions for both diabetes and obesity indications
- **Revenue acceleration**: Novo Nordisk and Eli Lilly reporting >40% YoY revenue growth from GLP-1 segments

### Calculated Signals Summary

- Novo/Lilly revenue acceleration from GLP-1 segment: Multi-billion dollar quarterly run rates
- Prescription volume growth: Double-digit monthly growth in new prescriptions
- Pipeline expansion: Next-gen oral GLP-1s, combination therapies, new indications (MASH, cardiovascular)

### Evidence Container Reference

`evidence.narrative.glp1_obesity_medicine` (CANDIDATE reference — not an evidence object)

### Provenance Readiness

- **Primary sources**: FDA filings (NDA approvals, label expansions), Phase 3 clinical study publications (NEJM, Lancet)
- **Secondary sources**: Company earnings disclosures (Novo Nordisk, Eli Lilly quarterly reports)
- **Data quality**: High — FDA-regulated data, peer-reviewed clinical evidence, audited financial disclosures

### Contradiction Review

- Safety signal emergence: potential cardiovascular, thyroid, pancreatitis concerns under long-term monitoring
- Insurance coverage pushback: payers limiting access due to high cost ($1,000+/month list price)
- Supply constraints: manufacturing capacity unable to meet demand (Novo capacity expansion ongoing)
- Discontinuation rates: weight regain upon stopping treatment raises durability questions
- Concentrated market: only 2 major manufacturers currently (Novo Nordisk, Eli Lilly)

### Evidence Limitations

- Market concentrated in 2 companies; individual company performance ≠ narrative validation
- Market access uncertain: insurance coverage decisions could limit TAM realization
- Long-term (10+ year) safety/efficacy data not yet available
- Revenue growth partially reflects supply catch-up, not steady-state demand
- Pipeline risk: next-gen candidates may fail, altering competitive dynamics

### Credit Solvency Relevance

Not applicable — scientific breakthrough narrative, not valuation-driven
